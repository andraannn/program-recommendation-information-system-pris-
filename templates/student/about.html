About the Project


Course Recommendation Information System

The Course Recommendation Information System is designed to assist students in selecting courses that align with their academic profiles and strengths. Developed by the Center for Computational Analytics and Modeling (CCAM) at the Premier Research Institute of Science and Mathematics (PRISM), this innovative system employs data-driven analysis and clustering techniques to understand student demographics and academic indicators.

By analyzing factors such as high school performance, socioeconomic background, entrance exam scores, and other relevant data, the system generates personalized course recommendations that align with students' strengths. This approach not only enhances students' academic experiences but also supports their long-term success by ensuring they are placed in courses where they are more likely to excel.

This project is funded by the Office of the Vice Chancellor for Research and Enterprise (OVCRE), demonstrating MSU-IIT’s commitment to advancing research and educational technology. Through initiatives like this, MSU-IIT strives to foster a more inclusive and effective learning environment.
